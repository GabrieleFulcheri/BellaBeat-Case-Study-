Structure of the analysis

Since my focus is on Bellabeats fitness watch, I selected three main dataset among those available, namely the data regarding daily activity, hourly activity and sleep. Therefore, the analysis is divided in three parts, one for each of the aforementioned type of data.

Key aspects of the data available and aim of the analysis

The dataset contains data regarding the activity of 33 different users. There are two main problems:

  • 33 people could be not representative enough of the population Bellabeat wants to target with its marketing

  • Notwithstanding the fact that Bellabeats products are designed for women, we havent gender related information (as well as other important demographic information such as age and country)

Moreover, since we havent data regarding the customers preferences and opinions about fitness products, the aim of my analysis will be to segment the 33 customers, in order to provide the basis for a future inquiry on each segment preferences and opinion about fitness watches.

wssplot <- function(data, nc=15, seed=1234){
  wss <- (nrow(data)-1)*sum(apply(data,2,var))
  for (i in 2:nc){
    set.seed(seed)
    wss[i] <- sum(kmeans(data, centers=i)$withinss)}
  plot(1:nc, wss, type="b", xlab="Number of Clusters",
       ylab="Within groups sum of squares")
  wss
}  ## to obtain the ideal number of cluster for the k-mean clustering

  • Cluster analysis has revealed that customers are mainly segmented into two parts: active to very active customers and very sedentary ones. It is up to the manager to decided which segment to target, but it is certain that two completely different marketing campaign need to be designed

  • Considering the hourly activity, it seems that, on average, customers have a standard routine that allows them to do fitness related activity mainly between 17:00 and 19:00. This is also confirmed by the analysis on sleep habit. If this is true, Bellabeat advertisement and placement strategies could focus on how fitness is related to working and studying routines

  • Since tracking sleep is not popular among customers, Bellabeat could insist on the importance of tracking sleep in order to have a complete insight on our personal wellness.
